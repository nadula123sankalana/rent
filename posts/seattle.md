## 1. Housing Market: Seattle vs. Suburbs
Rent prices in **Seattle** remain elevated in 2026.
* **The Premium Areas:** Living in **South Lake Union** or **Capitol Hill** commands a premium, with 1-bedrooms averaging **$3,050**.
* **The Budget Move:** By moving 25 minutes out to **Renton**, you can drop your rent to **$1,950**. This is the most common strategy for young professionals here.

## 2. Utility Costs & Winter Heat
Residents often underestimate the cost of heating in Seattle.
* **Peak Season:** In January, bills from **Seattle City Light** can spike to **$240**.
* **Water/Trash:** Usually adds another **$50/month** to your bill.

## 3. Transportation: Car vs. Public Transit
Seattle is a mix of car use and transit.
* **Car Insurance:** Drivers in Washington pay approximately **$190** per month due to congestion and rain-related claims.
* **Public Transit:** The **Link Light Rail** is reliable along core corridors. A monthly pass costs **$99**.

## 4. Food & Entertainment
* **Groceries:** Expect to spend **$520** per month shopping at chains like **QFC** and **Safeway**.
* **Dining Out:** A dinner for two in **Capitol Hill** typically runs **$60-$80**.

### FAQ: Living in Seattle
**Q: Is Seattle expensive for singles?**
A: Compared to Portland, it is more expensive. A single person needs roughly **$4,100** post-tax.

**Q: What is the main hidden cost?**
A: Toll bridges on SR 520 and parking fees.
