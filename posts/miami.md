## 1. Housing Market: Miami vs. Suburbs
Rent prices in **Miami** remain elevated in 2026.
* **The Premium Areas:** Living in **Brickell** or **Wynwood** commands a premium, with 1-bedrooms averaging **$3,200**.
* **The Budget Move:** By moving 25 minutes out to **Kendall**, you can drop your rent to **$2,100**. This is the most common strategy for young professionals here.

## 2. Utility Costs & Humidity
Residents often underestimate the cost of air conditioning in Miami.
* **Peak Season:** In August, bills from **Florida Power & Light (FPL)** can spike to **$290**.
* **Water/Trash:** Usually adds another **$50/month** to your bill.

## 3. Transportation: Car vs. Public Transit
Miami is a car-dependent city.
* **Car Insurance:** Drivers in Florida pay approximately **$230** per month due to high traffic and hurricane risk.
* **Public Transit:** The **Metrorail** system is limited. A monthly pass costs **$112.50**.

## 4. Food & Entertainment
* **Groceries:** Expect to spend **$520** per month shopping at chains like **Publix** and **Winn-Dixie**.
* **Dining Out:** A dinner for two in **Wynwood** typically runs **$60-$80**.

### FAQ: Living in Miami
**Q: Is Miami expensive for singles?**
A: Compared to Orlando, it is more expensive. A single person needs roughly **$3,900** post-tax.

**Q: What is the main hidden cost?**
A: Parking fees and higher auto insurance.
