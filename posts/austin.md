## 1. Housing Market: Austin vs. Suburbs
Rent prices in **Austin** have cooled slightly in 2026.
* **The Premium Areas:** Living in **Downtown** or **Rainey Street** commands a premium, with 1-bedrooms averaging **$2,550**.
* **The Budget Move:** By moving 20 minutes out to **Round Rock**, you can drop your rent to **$1,750**. This is the most common strategy for young professionals here.

## 2. Utility Costs & Summer Heat
Residents often underestimate the cost of air conditioning in Austin.
* **Peak Season:** In August, bills from **Austin Energy** can spike to **$280**.
* **Water/Trash:** Usually adds another **$50/month** to your bill.

## 3. Transportation: Car vs. Public Transit
Austin is a car-dependent city.
* **Car Insurance:** Drivers in Texas pay approximately **$185** per month due to heavy traffic on I-35 and frequent hail claims.
* **Public Transit:** The **CapMetro** system is limited. A monthly pass costs **$41.25**.

## 4. Food & Entertainment
* **Groceries:** Expect to spend **$470** per month shopping at chains like **H-E-B** and **Whole Foods**.
* **Dining Out:** A dinner for two on **South Congress** typically runs **$60-$80**.

### FAQ: Living in Austin
**Q: Is Austin expensive for singles?**
A: Compared to Dallas, it is slightly more expensive. A single person needs roughly **$3,500** post-tax.

**Q: What is the main hidden cost?**
A: Toll roads on MoPac Express and SH 130.
