## 1. Housing Market: Dallas vs. Suburbs
Rent prices in **Dallas** have stabilized in 2026.
* **The Premium Areas:** Living in **Uptown** or **Deep Ellum** commands a premium, with 1-bedrooms averaging **$2,400**.
* **The Budget Move:** By moving 20 minutes out to **Plano**, you can drop your rent to **$1,750**. This is the most common strategy for young professionals here.

## 2. Utility Costs & Summer Heat
Residents often underestimate the cost of air conditioning in Dallas.
* **Peak Season:** In July, bills from **Oncor Electric Delivery** can spike to **$260**.
* **Water/Trash:** Usually adds another **$50/month** to your bill.

## 3. Transportation: Car vs. Public Transit
Dallas is a car-dependent city.
* **Car Insurance:** Drivers in Texas pay approximately **$185** per month due to heavy traffic on I-35 and the Dallas North Tollway.
* **Public Transit:** The **DART** system is limited. A monthly pass costs **$96**.

## 4. Food & Entertainment
* **Groceries:** Expect to spend **$480** per month shopping at chains like **Kroger** and **Tom Thumb**.
* **Dining Out:** A dinner for two in **Knox/Henderson** typically runs **$60-$80**.

### FAQ: Living in Dallas
**Q: Is Dallas expensive for singles?**
A: Compared to Austin, it is cheaper. A single person needs roughly **$3,400** post-tax.

**Q: What is the main hidden cost?**
A: Toll roads on the Dallas North Tollway and SH 121.
